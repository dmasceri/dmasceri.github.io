How I Got Here

I started out in applied structural engineering research, designing and evaluating bridges. What hooked me was the feedback loop: instrument a real bridge, test it under load, then tune a simulation until the model behaved like the structure standing in front of me. It became a dissertation — and a software library that automated the whole build, tune, and simulate loop engineers had been doing by hand.

Automating that loop for other engineers is what pulled me into product. I've been building software ever since at start-ups, mid-market B2B SaaS, and enterprise platforms. What never changed during the journey was the puzzle underneath it all. I take what people do by feel and hard-won experience, break it down into its essence, and put the pieces back together into something intuitive and automated. Along the way I found that people and teams are another kind of puzzle I got really good at solving: understand the parts, see how they fit, and make something great by bringing the pieces together.
