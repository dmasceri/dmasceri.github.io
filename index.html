RAMPS

Research archive

Rapid Automated Modeling for the Performance of Structures

MATLAB software that builds and load-rates finite-element models of highway bridges by driving the Strand7 solver through its API — sizing girders to AASHTO code, generating the 3D model, running simulated dead- and live-load tests, and computing rating factors with no manual modeling. Built during my doctoral research at Drexel and matured at Rutgers as the software companion to the THMPR rapid-testing system (European patent; ASCE Pankow Award). Why I made it: together the two compressed a full field assessment of a bridge from roughly a week down to a single day. It's archived here as written — the interesting part is what it does, not how pretty it is.

MORPHFLIGHT

Playable prototype

On-rails cave flyer with a morph-weapon core verb

You auto-fly forward through a cavernous stone hall, dodging and aiming with the stick while reshaping your weapon's spread — orb, horizontal disk, vertical disk — to match the enemy formation in front of you. Match the shape and the formation melts and a chain builds; mismatch and it only chips. Why I made it: to prove out one idea — that a two-layer "aim and morph" verb could carry a shooter — and lock the design decisions before any polish. This repo is the runnable vertical slice from that prototyping phase.

Intraday Regime Dashboard

Working tool

Read-only market regime & intraday observation dashboard

A calibration and observation tool for intraday equity metrics (SPY / QQQ / IWM) against an Alpaca paper account and free FRED data — no orders, no positions. An engine process owns all the data I/O and metric math and writes a permanent calibration log to SQLite; a thin Streamlit UI just reads and renders, and stays honest about staleness when the engine is down. Why I made it: I wanted to watch how regime signals behave through a live session and build intuition before ever risking a dollar. Ships as a two-process Docker stack.
