A bit more

I started out in applied structural engineering research, designing and evaluating bridges. What hooked me was the feedback loop: instrument a real bridge, test it under load, then tune a simulation until the model behaved like the structure standing in front of me. It became a dissertation — and a software library that automated the whole build, tune, and simulate loop engineers had been doing by hand.

Automating that loop for other engineers is what pulled me into product. I've been building software ever since — through startups, mid-market B2B SaaS, IoT, and now enterprise platforms — but the puzzle underneath never changed: take something people do by feel, all heuristics and hard-won experience, break it down to its essence, and fit the pieces back together into something simple enough to automate. And it turns out people and teams are the same kind of puzzle — understand the parts, see how they fit, and alignment becomes something you can actually solve for.
